Example: Compare a regular polygon with 4 sides, 5 side length and a 5 apothem to a rectangle with a length of 5 and a width of 10. Both are 50 in area.

For all equivalent triangles, the equilateral tirnagle has the smallest perimeter.
Example: Compare a triangle with a height of 3 and all sides 3 to a right triangle with a base and height of 3. Both are 4.5 in area.

For all equivalent quadrilaterals, the square has the smallest perimeter.
Example: Compare a square with a side length of 5 to a rectangle with a length of 25 and a height of 1. Both are 25 in area.

For all rectangular prisms with the same surface area, the cube is the one with the largest volume
Example: Compare a cube with a side length of 5 to a rectangular prism with a length of 5, a width of 3, and a height of 7.5. Both are 150 in surface area.

For all rectangular prisms with the same volume, the cube is the one with the smallest surface area.
Example: Compare a cube with a side length of 4 to a rectangular prism with a length of 2, a width of 4, and a height of 8. Both are 64 in volume..
